Selection Criteria For Managed Succession


[1] Managed succession complies with agency state or regional roadside vegetation management plan.
[2] The project location has enough ROW width beyond safety clear zone to eliminate a minimum of one full mow pass (approximate minimum 15’).
[3] The project location has enough median ROW width beyond safety clear zone to eliminate a minimum of one full mow pass (approximate minimum 15’).
[4] The project location has enough interchange ROW area beyond the dafety clear zone to eliminate a minimum of on full mow pass (approximate minimum 15').
[5] Larger/taller vegetation impact on safety/performance/maintenance/viability of the following:
    [5a] Median barrier
    [5b] Sight distance and/or conflicts at access points in the urban/suburban context for vehicles, bicycles, pedestrians, etc.
    [5c] Sight distance issues due to roadway geometry (i.e., curves and changing elevation)
    [5d] Existing signs, signals, luminaire supports, overhead or underground utilities, and similar roadside features
    [5e] Prevalence of illegal/unauthorized use of the ROW or other safety related issues (particularly urban/suburban)
    [5f] Drainage structures
    [5g] Vegetation requirements for pollinators/wildlife habitat conservation and/or habitat establishment
    [5h] Cooperative agreements/volunteer groups for other uses of the ROW such as hay and timber harvesting, support for pollinators/wildlife
    [5i] Local agency resolution/cooperative agreement ability to maintain the site using their personnel, equipment, etc.
    [5j] Management of drifting/blowing snow
    [5k] Winter maintenance (i.e., snow melt drainage, snow storage, winter shadow)
[6] Larger/taller vegetation impact on safety/performance/viability of the roadway context and/or classification:
    [6a] Urban interstate, freeway or expressway
    [6b] Urban principal artertial
    [6c] Rural interate, freeway or expressway
    [6d] Rural low-volume road
[7] Larger/taller vegetation impact on or conflicts with the adjacent land use/landowner:
    [7a] Existing forest/heavily vegetated areas
    [7b] Agricultural
    [7c] Suburban/urban
    [7d] Commercial
    [7e] Industrial
    [7f] Residential
[8] Larger/taller vegetation impact as a fire hazard within ROW and beyond:
    [8a] Proximity of larger vegetation to structures
    [8b] Adjacent property fire hazard potential
    [8c] Regional climate – arid/semi-arid
    [8d] Historical fire data for specific location
