Criteria for a Modified Roadside Management Protocol

Departments of Transportation (DOT)s have historically incorporated roadside vegetation management operations that consist of mowing and herbicide treatments not only the safety clear zones, but also the remainder of the right-of-way (ROW) from boundary to boundary. These roadside vegetation management practices have come into question as being a reasonable maintenance and operations methodology when considering cost and environmental concerns versus benefit. DOTs are re-evaluating their roadside management practices and seeking answers to support minimizing ROW maintenance without compromising safety.

This Interactive Tool provides insight into the complexities of roadside vegetation management and how modifications to established vegetation management practices may provide increased benefits regarding worker safety, environmental sustainability, and cost savings for DOTs. Roadside maintenance and operation parameters accommodate different types of adjacent development and users such as urban, industrial and agricultural. The managed succession method of roadside maintenance uses a strategic approach of selective control measures such as zero maintenance, targeted mowing, mechanical trimming and removal, and chemical and/or biological treatments to allow plant species to colonize roadside areas outside the safety clear zone. Often these plant species are larger and woodier. As such, these plant materials may provide habitat for pollinators and other wildlife. This approach is typically part of a long-term plan to minimize ROW maintenance requirements over time.

The user will be prompted to input the following information:

  • Project Name
  • Project Location
  • Current vegetation management practice (e.g., routine mowing, reduced mowing)
  • Project Start Date
  • Project Description
  • User Name
  • User Email

The Interactive Tool is designed to allow the user to assess each statement presented as it relates to site conditions and applicability to managed succession. The user will determine of the criteria presented is a critical concern that may preclude managed succession, is a concern but manageable, or is either N/A or no conflict as shown in Table 1 below. Responses will be evaluated for a recommendation for implementation of managed succession. The Interactive Tool will produce a pdf containing the project information input with a recommendation.
